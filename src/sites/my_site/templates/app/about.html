I am a computational linguist. I work at the LINGUIST List. I studied computer science and linguistics at Indiana University in Bloomington, Indiana University-Purdue University in Fort Wayne, Asian Institute of Technology in Bangkok.

I worked in Burma, Laos and Thailand before working at LINGUIST List. My past experience includes doing research at the Center for Research in Computational Linguistics (Southeast Asian Languages Archive) and teaching at the Myanmar Institute of Theology.

I am working and have worked on some of these projects:

  • Endangered Languages Catalog: a worldwide effort to share knowledge and resources for endangered languages
  • MultiTree: a searchable database of hypotheses on language relationships
  • Southeast Asian Linguistics Archives
  • SEALang Burmese, Mon, Shan and Karen dictionaries
  • SEALang Thai, Khmer and Lao dictionaries

I enjoy working on projects that encompass mathematics, computer science and linguistics. I wrote my Master's thesis on the Automatic Bitext Alignment for Southeast Asian Languages. I also like music and travelling. I play guitar and sing during my leisure. I love travelling to Southeast Asian countries and learning about their languages.
